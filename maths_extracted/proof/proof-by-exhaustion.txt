Proof by exhaustion
What is proof by exhaustion?
Proof by exhaustion is a way to show that the desired
result works for every allowed value
How do I prove a result by
exhaustion?
Using proof by exhaustion means testing every allowed
value not just showing a few examples

Difficulties with proof by
exhaustion
In many cases proof by exhaustion is not practical, or
possible
Proving all multiples of 4 are even can’t be shown for
every multiple of 4
Aim to minimise the work involved
Proving a number is prime only requires testing
factors up to the square root
Examiner Tips and Tricks
Try a simpler case if you are stuck.
For example, if you are asked to prove that 97 is a prime
number you could try thinking about what you would do
for smaller primes such as 7 or 11.
