Separation of variables
What does it mean for a differential
equation to be separable?
Many differential equations used in modelling have two
variables involved (ie x and y)
If there is a product of functions in different variables, the
differential equation is separable
ie dy/dx = f(x) × g(y)

Got it
Differential equations of the form dy/dx=g(y) should be
thought of as dy/dx=1 × g(y)
where f(x) = 1
How do I solve a differential
equation using separation of
variables?
STEP 1: Separate all y terms on one side and all x terms on
the other side
STEP 2: Integrate both sides
STEP 3: Include one “overall” constant of integration
STEP 4: Use the initial or boundary condition to find the
particular solution
STEP 5: Write the particular solution in sensible, or
required, format
