How do I find the area between a
curve and a line by separating into
two areas?
The area enclosed will be the difference between …
the area under the curve and
the area under the line
These can be found separately
There may be easier ways to find the area under a line
Once the areas are found a question can be answered in
full
STEP 1: Find the intersections of the line and the curve
STEP 2: Find the area under a curve, R , using definite
C
integration
STEP 3: Find the area under a line, R , either using definite
L
integration or the area formulae for basic shapes
STEP 4: To find the area, R, between the curve and the line
subtract the smaller area from the larger area
If curve on top this will be R – R
C L
If line on top this will be R - R
L C
