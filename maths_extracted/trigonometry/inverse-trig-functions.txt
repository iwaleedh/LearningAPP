Inverse trigonometric
functions
What are the inverse trigonometric
functions?
These functions are the inverse functions of sin, cos and
tan
sin(arcsin x) = x
cos(arccos x) = x
tan(arctan x) = x
The domains of sin, cos, and tan must first be restricted to
make them one-to-one functions (only one-to-one
functions have inverses)
What are the restricted domains?
domain of sin x is restricted to -π/2 ≤ x ≤ π/2

Examiner Tips and Tricks
Make sure you know the shapes of the graphs for sin, cos
and tan.
As inverses, the graphs of arcsin, arccos and arctan are
reflections of sin, cos and tan in the line y = x.
The values returned by the sin-1, cos-1 and tan-1 keys on
your calculator are the values from the ranges of arcsin,
arccos and arctan.
