How do I solve quadratic
trigonometric equations?
If an equation involves sin2θ or cos2θ then it is a quadratic
trigonometric equation
These can be solved by factorising and/or using
trigonometric identities (see Trigonometry – Simple
Identities)
As a quadratic can result in two solutions, you will need to
consider whether each solution exists and then find all
solutions within a given interval for each
You may be asked to use degrees or radians to solve
trigonometric equations

Examiner Tips and Tricks
Sketch the appropriate sin, cos, tan graph to ensure you
find ALL solutions within the given interval, and be super-
careful if you get a negative solution but have a positive
interval.
For example, for an equation, in the interval 0° ≤ x ≤ 360°,
with solution sin x = -¼ then sin-1(-¼) = -14.5 (to 1d.p.),
which is not between 0 and 360 – by sketching the graph
you’ll be able to spot the two solutions will be 180 + 14.5
and 360 - 14.5.
