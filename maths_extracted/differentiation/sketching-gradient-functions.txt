Examiner Tips and Tricks
If f(x) is a smooth curve then f'(x) will also be a smooth
curve.
Take what you know about f'(x) (based on the table
above) and then 'fill in the blanks' in between.
If all you have is the graph of f(x) you will not be able to
specify the coordinates of the y-intercept or any
stationary points of f'(x).
Be careful – points where f(x) cuts the x-axis don't tell you
anything about the graph of f'(x)!
