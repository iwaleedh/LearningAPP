What is a sequence?
A sequence is an ordered set of numbers with a rule for
finding all the numbers in the sequence
The numbers in a sequence are called terms
Stuck on something?
The terms of a sequence are often referred to by letters
with a subscript Ask questions and get help with
exactly what you're revising.

What is a series?
You get a series by summing up the terms in a sequence
We use the notation S to refer to the sum of the first n

What are increasing, decreasing
and periodic sequences?
A sequence is increasing if u > u for all positive
n+1 n
integers n
i.e. if every term is greater than the term before it
A sequence is decreasing if u < u for all positive
n+1 n
integers n
i.e. if every term is less than the term before it
A sequence is periodic if the terms repeat in a cycle
The order (or period) of a periodic sequence is the
number of terms in each repeating cycle
Examiner Tips and Tricks
Look out for sequences defined by trigonometric functions –
this can be a way of 'hiding' a periodic function.
