Arithmetic series
How do I find the sum of an
arithmetic series?
An arithmetic series is the sum of the terms of an
arithmetic sequence
Help me understand
The following formulae will let you find the sum of the first n
terms of an arithmetic series:
n n
S = (2a+(n−1)d) or S = (a+l)
n 2 n 2
a is the first term
d is the common difference
l is the last term Stuck on something?

How do I prove the arithmetic series
formula?
Learn this proof of the arithmetic series formula – you can
be asked to give it on the exam:
Write the terms out once in order
Write the terms out again in reverse order
Add the two sums together
The terms will pair up to give the same sum
2a+(n−1)d
There will be n of these terms
Divide by two as two of the sums have been added
together
Examiner Tips and Tricks
